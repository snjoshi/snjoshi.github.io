Hi, I'm Cassidy Williams! I'm a software engineer and developer evangelist at Clarifai. I like it there.

I started my voyage into building for the great unknown (commonly referred to as the World Wide Web) when I was an 8th grader in the suburbs of Chicago, and I've been loving it ever since. I'm primarily interested in front-end development, but hey, it's always fun to try other things too.

I graduated in May 2014 from Iowa State University with my degree in computer science and minor in Spanish. During my time there, I interned at several companies, including Intuit, Microsoft, and General Mills. I was in charge of the Computer Science/Software Engineering Club, among other things. I loved my time at ISU. But now, I'm in New York, which is fun and different!

After school, I joined Venmo in NYC, where I was also a software engineer and developer evangelist. While there, my work made Venmo's API the #1 payments API in the 2014-2015 hackathon season. I joined Clarifai in August 2015, where I'm working on building developer evangelism in the organization from the ground up. I'm also doing some front-end engineering work, as their first female engineer! Since moving to NYC, I've had the honor of working with various organizations, such as Hacker Fund as their Director of Outreach, and I've been included as one of Glamour Magazine's 35 Women Under 35 Who Are Changing the Tech Industry and in the Innotribe Power Women in FinTech Index. I've also had the pleasure of working with Microsoft on the Big Dream documentary and the U.S.A. Science and Engineering Festival with their Nifty Fifty program to encourage more students to participate in STEM.

One of my favorite things to do is help people be successful, whether it means mentoring them at a hackathon, editing their resume, or giving a talk. Check out the Outreach section to learn more about that. If you want.
